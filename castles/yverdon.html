Yverdon Castle

Yverdon-les-Bains Castle: A Gateway to Swiss History

Description

Situated in the heart of present-day Yverdon-les-Bains, the castle has seen numerous transformations throughout the centuries.

History

The Castle of Yverdon-les-Bains, recognized as the first "carré savoyard" defensive structure, is a lowland military fortress commissioned by Peter, Count of Savoy, in 1259, and designed by the architects John and James of Saint Georges, known as Master James.

Since 1915, the castle has served as the centerpiece of the Museum of Yverdon and its Region, offering a journey through nearly 8,000 years of local history. Its heritage extends far beyond Swiss borders.

Today, the castle also hosts the Pestalozzi Research and Documentation Centre, temporary exhibitions from the Swiss Museum of Fashion, and the Echandole Theatre, nestled within parts of the historic cellars.
