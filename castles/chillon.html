Chillon Castle

Chillon Castle: A Timeless Gem on Lake Geneva

Description

Nestled on the tranquil shores of Lake Geneva, Chillon Castle is one of Switzerland’s most iconic and well-preserved medieval fortresses. With over a thousand years of history, this remarkable castle has served as a strategic stronghold, a residence for the Counts of Savoy, and an inspiration for writers and artists alike.

Visitors are transported back in time as they explore Chillon’s ancient halls, dungeons, and stunning courtyards. The breathtaking views of the lake and the surrounding Alps add to the castle’s allure, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs, architecture enthusiasts, and nature lovers.

Inside, discover the grand banquet halls, intricate frescoes, and the famous underground vaults that once held political prisoners, including Lord Byron’s muse for The Prisoner of Chillon. Whether you’re admiring its medieval architecture or enjoying the serene lakeside scenery, Chillon Castle offers a captivating blend of history, culture, and beauty.

History

Chillon Castle’s history dates back to the 10th century, though its strategic location on the rocky islet of Lake Geneva was used for centuries before that. It became a key fortress during the Middle Ages, serving as both a residence and defensive post for the Counts of Savoy. Over time, the castle evolved, with significant expansions and fortifications added in the 12th and 13th centuries.

During the 16th century, Chillon was captured by the Bernese during their conquest of the Vaud region, and it remained under Bernese control for nearly 260 years. In the 19th century, the castle gained literary fame when Lord Byron wrote The Prisoner of Chillon, inspired by a political prisoner once held in its dungeons.

Today, Chillon Castle is a beautifully preserved heritage site, offering visitors a glimpse into medieval life while showcasing its role in Swiss and European history.

Visitors information

  • For visits without a guide there is an explanatory brochure available for individual visitors in the following languages: Czech, Dutch, English, French, German, Hebrew, Hungarian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Mandarin, Polish, Portuguese, Russian, Spanish,Thai and Arabic.

  • Audioguide available in 9 languages: French, German, English, Spanish, Italian, Russian, Japanese, Chinese and Korean can be rented on site or downloaded from the App Store or Google Play.

  • An outdoor area with view on the lake is available for picnics. A small snack bar sells drinks, ice creams and snacks cravings visitors may have before or after their visit.

  • Baby-changing tables are available for parents upon request. Strollers must be parked in the indoor rest area. Please opt for a baby-carrier for your visit of the castle.

  • Toilets for the handicapped are available upon request.

  • Dogs are not permitted inside the castle (even in a carrier). Kennels and places to leave them are available just outside.

  • Most of the castle is not easily accessible for visitors in wheelchairs. However, there are interactive terminals in the rest area which provide a virtual visit of the castle.
