Meggenhorn Castle

Meggenhorn Castle: A Fairytale Retreat by Lake Lucerne

Description

Situated on the picturesque shores of Lake Lucerne, Castle Meggenhorn is a stunning example of 19th-century architecture, surrounded by lush gardens and breathtaking views. This romantic castle, built in the neo-Gothic style, stands as a testament to the opulence of its era. With its distinctive turrets and ornate balconies, Meggenhorn captivates visitors with its fairy-tale charm.

Originally constructed in 1850 as a private residence for a wealthy merchant, the castle has since transformed into a popular destination for events and gatherings. The interior features beautifully preserved rooms that showcase the elegance of the period, complete with intricate woodwork and antique furnishings.

Visitors to Castle Meggenhorn can explore the scenic grounds, enjoy leisurely walks along the lake, or partake in guided tours to learn more about its fascinating history. Whether you're an architecture enthusiast, a history buff, or simply seeking a serene escape, Castle Meggenhorn offers a magical experience amidst stunning natural beauty.

History

The castle in its present form was built in 1868-70 for the Mulhouse industrialist Edouad Hofer-Grosjean. In 1886, Countess Amélie Heine-Kohn bought the castle and had the neo-Gothic chapel built.

In 1920, Jakob Heinrich Frey-Baumann, an industrial textile builder from Zurich, bought the property. In 1960, following the death of their parents, their two daughters inherited Meggenhorn Castle. In 1974 the property became the property of the municipality.
